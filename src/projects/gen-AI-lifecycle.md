## Project Overview

The Lifecycle of a Generative AI query is a one-year fellowship-funded project (by [CEEDS](https://www.smith.edu/discover-smith/smith-action/sustainable-smith/center-environment-ecological-design-sustainability) the Center for the Environment, Ecological Design and Sustainability at Smith College) designed to show the wide impact of generative AI in our communities.  Through interviews, network mapping, sound and video recordings, this project will culminate in an interactive art installation that walks viewers through the people, places and sounds involved in a single generative AI query.

## Research Questions

- How can we tell the story of one generative AI query in a way that takes viewers through each step?
- How might we describe each step in a way that viewers have a felt and embodied sense of it?

## Methodology

#### Network tracing
We use standard forensic network tooks to understand the paths that our internet requests (including generative AI requests) take across servers, through data centers, and back to our laptops

#### Interviews and Ethnography
We're talking with people who interact with data centers (resisters, neighbors, employees) and those who use generative AI in their daily lives.


## Outputs

In the Spring of 2027, we'll build an installation on Smith Campus, combining qualitative and quantitative data with an art installation fabricated by Smith students under the supervision of the Black Sound Lab at Dartmouth College.
