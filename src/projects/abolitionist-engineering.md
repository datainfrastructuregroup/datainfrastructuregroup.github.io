## Project Overview

<i>Abolitionist Engineering</i> is a book-in-progress that:
- situates software, AI and data science as inseperable from the United States carceral state
- argues for abolitionist theory as a grounded approach to making technology
- makes specific technical recommendations for abolitionist technical practice

## Research Questions

- How, fundamentally, is AI distinct from software?
- Given that difference, what kinds of open source/community governance models might AI's unique properties require?
- What levels of openness (open weight, open data set, open source code) are most beneficial to community control over models?

## Methodology

#### Archival and Original Source/Code Research
The book examines original documents from government repositories, library collections, and community archives to narrate the development of carceral technological practices

#### Theoretical Research
The book also engages in deep theoretical development to argue for an abolitionist technical practice.
