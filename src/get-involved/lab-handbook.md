## Mission
Our official mission: We are an interdisciplinary research group that uses <i>computational</i> and <i>humanistic</i> methods to study and practice restorative and transformative data science.  

In practice, this means that we have a roster of active <a href="/projects/">projects</a> that investigate the social and cultural aspects of data and data infrastructures.  Because of <a href="http://www.nikkostevens.com">Nikko's</a> research, the lab has a geographical and temporal focus on the present-day United States. In other words: we study US-based data infrastructures and their recent development (~1960s - present).Occasionally we work on international projects, but only those with ties to US-based data infrastructures or data/software practices.

## Who we are
DIG is a community made of scholars from a variety of disciplines, who bring diverse perspectives and skillsets to our work.  We are committed to being an inclusive space for everyone interested in how data and data infrastructures shape our world. 

## Lab Membership
Joining the lab as a <u>community member</u> means that you're committing to:
- attending at least one lab meeting per academic year (we know scheduling is tough!)
- participating on Slack when you can (including sharing things you find that are relevant to our work)
- treating other lab members with respect
- following the <a href="https://www.smith.edu/your-campus/offices-services/office-student-affairs/student-handbook/code-student-conduct">Smith College Student Code of Conduct</a> (if you're a Smith student)

Some students will have the opportunity to join the lab as paid researchers. For more information, see <a href="/get-involved/student-hiring/">our student hiring</a>.
